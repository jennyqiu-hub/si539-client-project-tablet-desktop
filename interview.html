Interview Support

Prepare with a Clear Plan

Interviews evaluate both your skills and how you communicate your experience. This page breaks the process into practical steps, with quick paths for different interview formats and resources you can use to practice.

If you want feedback, consider scheduling time with a career coach: Talk to a Career Coach .

Start Here

  1. Identify your interview type: behavioral, technical, case, or a mix.
  2. Collect 5–7 stories: projects, teamwork, leadership, failure, conflict, impact.
  3. Practice out loud: time yourself, record once, then refine.
  4. Prepare questions: ask about team workflow, success metrics, and onboarding.

Common Interview Formats

Behavioral Interviews

Focus on how you worked in real situations. Prepare concise stories with context, actions, and outcomes.

Technical / Skill-Based Interviews

Expect role-relevant tasks (e.g., product thinking, design critique, analytics, coding, or tool-based exercises). Practice explaining your thinking clearly as you work.

Case / Scenario Interviews

You may be asked to respond to a scenario (e.g., improving a feature, designing a system, or diagnosing an issue). Structure your answer: goals, constraints, assumptions, approach, trade-offs.

Interview FAQ

How long should my answers be?

Aim for 60–120 seconds for most behavioral answers. Prioritize clarity over detail.

What if I don’t have internship experience?

Use class projects, personal projects, team work, volunteering, or part-time work. Focus on what you did and what changed because of it.

How do I handle “Tell me about yourself”?

Use a simple structure: present focus (now), relevant past, and why this role next.
